Devendra Fadnavis is the new CM of Maharashtra.Devendra Sarita Gangadharrao Fadnavis(born 22 July 1970) is an Indian politician and currently serving Chief Minister of Maharashtra since 5 December 2024. He previously served as the Deputy Chief Minister of Maharashtra, alongside Ajit Pawar in the Eknath Shinde government. He previously held the position of Chief Minister of Maharashtra from 31 October 2014 to 12 November 2019. From 2019 to 2022, he served as the Leader of the Opposition in the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly. He was the President of BJP's Maharashtra state unit from 2013 to 2015.[4]

Eknath shinde is the new Deputy CM of Maharashtra.Eknath Sambhaji shinde born on 9 February 1964 is an Indian politician who is serving as the Deputy Chief Minister of Maharashtra since December 2024 alongside Ajit Pawar. He is the chairperson of Shiv Sena[4] since February 2023 and the former Chief Minister of Maharashtra, serving from June 2022 to December 2024. He is also the Member of Legislative Assembly for the Kopri-Pachpakhadi constituency[5] of Thane, Maharashtra, since 2009 and was formerly the MLA of Thane constituency from 2004 to 2009.

Ajit pawar serving as the Deputy CM of Maharashtra along with shinde.Ajit Anantrao Pawar[2] (Marathi pronunciation: [əd͡ʒit̪ pəʋaːɾ]; born 22 July 1959)[3] is an Indian politician from Maharashtra serving as the Current Deputy Chief Minister of Maharashtra along with Eknath Shinde since 5 December 2024 . He previously served as the Deputy Chief Minister of Maharashtra in the previous Eknath Shinde government.[4] He served as the Leader of the Opposition in the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly from 2022 to 2023 and represented Baramati Lok Sabha constituency in 1991.[5][6] He served as deputy chief minister many times.[7]

NARENDRA MODI

Narendra Damodardas Modi[a] (born 17 September 1950)[b] is an Indian politician who has served as Prime Minister of India since 2014. Modi was the chief minister of Gujarat from 2001 to 2014 and is the member of parliament (MP) for Varanasi. He is a member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and of the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), a right-wing Hindu nationalist paramilitary volunteer organisation. He is the longest-serving prime minister outside the Indian National Congress.[4]
